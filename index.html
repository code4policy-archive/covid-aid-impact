Assessing The Impact of COVID on International Aid Flows

Covid has reshuffled the international aid flows. But where is the money going? How is it affecting development projects and traditional sectors of investment? This data-driven map will allow you to study aid flow trends before and after Covid and measure its impact on different stakeholders and investment sectors.

AID FLOWS

The below dashboard visualizes the aid flows from two of the biggest aid providers - the United States Federal Government and Asian Development Bank (ADB). These two institutions account for over two-thirds of all aid flows made in USD in the last decade from 2010-2020. Take a look below to see how international aid across the world from these two institutions has changed over time. You can also explore how aid to a recipient country has changed in light of COVID and which sectors have the aid in 2020 went to. Tap the bottom right corner of the dashboard to view it full-screen.

TRENDS

Most countries have seen a decrease in the number of aid projects in 2020 compared to the usual number in the previous years


More than 70% of countries that have received aid in 2019 have seen a decrease in development projects in 2020. While the COVID pandemic may not directly cause this, the significantly high number of countries that have seen this trend is undeniable. There has also been a similar trend in the number of commitments and disbursements given to governments.

Data from the IATI database. D3 visualization from here.



On the country level, it can reverse or continue a trend, both leading to a set back in development and impacting lives


Sierra Leone just recently suffered a massive cut in aid, which experts say led to the spread of the deadly Ebola virus. The country hoped to retrieve some of the aid but Covid led to an even further reduction in international aid

DATA SOURCES


Finding the right data source to work with can be tricky. For experts and beginner researchers alike, we recommend these selected top favorites for their credibility, volume, and interactivity.

  • International Aid Transparency Initiative Database

    This dataset covers data from over 1300 development organizations, including the OECD and The World bank. Data can be explored through the portal, and for very detailed searches â€“ for example, by country, by sector or by organization publishing data.

    Created by the International Aid Transparency Initiative.

  • Detailed aid statistics

    This is a library of data sources by the OECD that hosts several data sets including a comparative table total official flows by recipient, donor, aid type.

    Created by the OECD.

  • Foreignassistance.gov

    This data set presents a picture of U.S. foreign assistance collection, tracking, and publication of the full lifecycle of all USG foreign assistance data.

    Created by the US govermnent.

  • Development Finance Data

    Presents a detailed picture of which country of the ODA spends what, and where.

    Created by the OECD'S Development Assistance Committee.

  • World Bank Open Data

    Extensive data to on development data divided by country or indicator.

    Created by the World Bank.
